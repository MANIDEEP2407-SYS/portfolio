Neural Tremor Suppression Sleeve Prototype

A wearable medical device designed for Parkinson's disease patients. It reads muscle tremor frequency in real-time using EMG sensors and MPU6050 (gyroscope/accelerometer), then delivers precise counter-vibrations at the exact opposing frequency to suppress involuntary tremors — providing non-invasive symptom relief. The sleeve continuously adapts its vibration output to match the patient's tremor pattern, improving motor control and quality of life.

MPU6050 EMG Sensors Embedded C Signal Processing

Water Salinity and its Impact in Kalpar Village

A Participatory Rural Appraisal Approach

An on-ground field research study conducted in Kalpar Village examining the relationship between water salinity levels and their socioeconomic and agricultural impacts on the local community. Provides data-driven insights and community-led recommendations for water quality intervention.
